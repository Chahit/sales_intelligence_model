================================================================================
MODULE: INVENTORY LIQUIDATION
FILE:   frontend/tabs/inventory.py
ENGINE: ml_engine/competitor_mixin.py + services/data_repository.py
================================================================================

PURPOSE
-------
Inventory Liquidation identifies "dead stock" — products sitting in the warehouse
for a long time with no or very slow movement — and surfaces the best
EXISTING PARTNERS to approach for clearing that stock.

Rather than selling dead stock to random buyers at a discount, this module
finds partners who have PREVIOUSLY purchased that product, giving the sales team
a warm call list with context.

Dead stock costs the company:
  - Storage space and handling costs
  - Risk of expiry, quality degradation, or obsolescence
  - Tied-up working capital that cannot be redeployed


WHAT IT SHOWS
-------------
1. Dead Stock Alert Banner   - Count of items needing attention
2. Product Selector          - Dropdown of all dead stock products
3. Stock Metrics:
   - Total Stock Left (units)
   - Max Age (days since stock was acquired)
   - Effective Age (accounting for last buyer activity)
   - Age Percentile (how old this is vs portfolio)
   - Priority Level (High / Medium / Low)
   - Exposure Score (0-100)
4. Target Buyers Table       - Past purchasers sorted by historical quantity
   - Partner Name, Contact, Past Qty, Last Purchase Date


ALGORITHMS & FORMULAS USED
---------------------------

A. DEAD STOCK IDENTIFICATION (view_ageing_stock)
-------------------------------------------------
Source: PostgreSQL view "view_ageing_stock"
Columns: product_name, total_stock_qty, max_age_days

A product qualifies as "dead stock" when:
  - max_age_days > 60 days (configurable)   AND
  - total_stock_qty > 10 units

The max_age_days is the age of the OLDEST batch of that product in stock.
If a product has multiple batches, the worst (oldest) is used.

View logic:
  max_age_days = current_date - MIN(date_received) for unsold units
  total_stock_qty = SUM(unsold quantity) across all batches


B. PRIORITY SCORING (get_stock_details)
-----------------------------------------
Each dead stock product receives a composite Priority Score (0-100):

Stock Exposure Score components:
  age_factor   = max_age_days / max_age_days_in_portfolio  (normalized 0-1)
  qty_factor   = total_stock_qty / max_qty_in_portfolio    (normalized 0-1)

  stock_exposure_score = (0.6 × age_factor + 0.4 × qty_factor) × 100

Priority levels:
  score >= 70  → "High Priority" (Liquidate Immediately)
  score >= 40  → "Medium Priority" (Plan Sales Campaign)
  score < 40   → "Low Priority" (Monitor)

Effective Age vs Max Age:
  If the product was last PURCHASED by any partner in the last 60 days
    → effective_age = days since last buyer activity 
    (there is still demand! reduce urgency slightly)
  Otherwise:
    → effective_age = max_age_days (no recent demand)

demand_recency_days = days since last partner purchased this product from us
age_percentile = percentile of this product's age vs entire portfolio


C. TARGET BUYER IDENTIFICATION (view_stock_liquidation_leads)
--------------------------------------------------------------
Source: PostgreSQL view "view_stock_liquidation_leads"

Logic:
  1. Find all partners who have EVER purchased this product
  2. Rank by their historical purchase quantity (desc)
  3. Return their contact number and last purchase date

Query structure:
  SELECT buyer_name, mobile_no, SUM(qty) as buyer_past_purchase_qty,
         MAX(date) as last_purchase_date
  FROM transactions
  WHERE product_name = :selected_product
  GROUP BY buyer_name, mobile_no
  ORDER BY buyer_past_purchase_qty DESC

This gives the sales team a PRIORITIZED outreach list — the top buyer for
dead SSD stock is most likely to want more SSD stock.

The "last_purchase_date" tells the rep how long ago the partner bought this,
helping them calibrate the conversation:
  < 3 months ago → "You recently bought X, we have more at a special rate"
  > 1 year ago   → "You used to carry X — here is a refresh opportunity"


D. DATA QUALITY CHECKS
------------------------
- If view_ageing_stock returns 0 rows: show green banner "No dead stock"
- If a product is in ageing stock but NOT in liquidation leads:
  stock exists but nobody historically bought it → show warning
  (this is truly dead stock with no natural buyer)


DATA FLOW
---------
  PostgreSQL → view_ageing_stock (df_stock_stats: product_name, qty, max_age_days)
             → inventory.py: read df_stock_stats for product list
             → get_stock_details(product): computes priority, exposure score
             → get_dead_stock(): reads view_stock_liquidation_leads
             → Filters to selected product → shows target buyer table


IMPROVEMENTS / SUGGESTIONS
---------------------------
1. DEMAND FORECASTING FOR CLEARANCE: Instead of just showing historical buyers,
   predict the PROBABILITY each buyer would purchase NOW based on their
   recent activity and the product's fit with their current buying pattern.

2. DISCOUNT OPTIMIZATION: Calculate the minimum discount needed to move the
   stock within a target time window, based on historical price elasticity.

3. BUNDLE CLEARANCE: Use MBA association rules to package dead stock products
   with fast-moving products as a bundle offer, protecting margin.

4. GEOGRAPHIC DEAD STOCK MAP: Show which warehouse location holds the dead stock
   and surface buyers in the nearest geography to minimize logistics cost.

5. AUTO-ALERTING: When a product crosses the 60-day threshold OR when the
   exposure score crosses 70, automatically send a task to the regional manager.

6. RETURN TO VENDOR: If no buyers exist and the product is approaching expiry,
   flag it for vendor return negotiation.

7. MARKDOWN CALENDAR: Suggest a structured 30-60-90 day markdown schedule
   (5% → 10% → 15%) rather than a single deep discount upfront.

8. INSURANCE / WRITE-OFF TRACKING: Track the financial impact of dead stock
   that was ultimately written off, to inform future purchasing decisions.
