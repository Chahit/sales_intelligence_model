PURPOSE
-------
Product Lifecycle Intelligence tracks where each product GROUP sits on the
standard business lifecycle curve (Introduction → Growth → Maturity → Decline)
and proactively flags:
  - Fast-growing products to stock up on and promote
  - Declining products approaching End-of-Life (EOL)
  - Cannibalization — when one product is rising while a close substitute falls
  - Revenue trajectory for every product group

This prevents the common scenario of over-ordering a product that is quietly
declining while under-investing in one that is surging.


WHAT IT SHOWS
-------------
1. Lifecycle Stage Summary   - Count of products in each stage
2. Velocity Leaderboard      - Top growing and declining products
3. Growth Stage Chart        - Bar chart of velocity scores
4. Cannibalization Pairs     - A is rising while B (its substitute) is falling
5. End-of-Life Predictions   - Products predicted to go to zero revenue
6. Product Trend Drilldown   - Monthly revenue sparkline per product
7. Urgency Flags             - Critical / Warning / Watch labels


ALGORITHMS & FORMULAS USED
---------------------------

A. DATA LOADING (_load_product_monthly_revenue)
------------------------------------------------
Aggregates transaction data by PRODUCT GROUP per month:
  SELECT group_name, DATE_TRUNC('month', date), SUM(net_amt), COUNT(DISTINCT party_id)
  FROM transactions
  GROUP BY group_name, month

Every product group gets a time series of:
  monthly_revenue      - net sales revenue per month
  buyer_count          - distinct partners buying that month
  transaction_count    - number of invoices

Lookback: configurable (default 18 months)


B. GROWTH VELOCITY SCORING (_compute_growth_velocity)
------------------------------------------------------
Four velocity signals are computed per product:

B1. Linear Regression Slope (Revenue Trend)
  Using month index as X (0, 1, 2, ..., N-1) and monthly_revenue as Y:
    slope, intercept = numpy.polyfit(X, Y, deg=1)
  
  slope_normalized = slope / mean_revenue
  → Positive = growing. Negative = declining.
  → Normalized by mean ensures fair comparison across products at different scales.

B2. 3-Month vs 3-Month-Prior Growth Rate
  recent_3m   = avg revenue in last 3 months
  prior_3m    = avg revenue in 3 months before that
  growth_rate = (recent_3m - prior_3m) / prior_3m   [clipped at ±5.0]

B3. Buyer Count Trend
  Same regression applied to buyer_count time series
  buyer_slope_norm = slope_of_buyer_count / mean_buyer_count

B4. Transaction Intensity Trend
  Same regression applied to transaction_count time series
  txn_slope_norm = slope_of_transaction_count / mean_transaction_count

COMBINED VELOCITY SCORE:
  velocity_score = (0.40 × slope_normalized)
                 + (0.35 × growth_rate)
                 + (0.15 × buyer_slope_norm)
                 + (0.10 × txn_slope_norm)

Final score normalized to [0, 1] across all products and then
converted to a symmetric scale where:
  > 0.6  → Strong growth
  0.4-0.6 → Stable
  < 0.4  → Declining

LIFECYCLE STAGE CLASSIFICATION:
  Based on velocity_score and revenue level:
  - "Star"     : velocity > 0.65, revenue above median → fast growth, high revenue
  - "Growth"   : velocity > 0.55 → growing
  - "Mature"   : velocity 0.40-0.55, stable revenue
  - "Declining": velocity < 0.40 → trend going negative
  - "Niche"    : low buyer count (<5) regardless of velocity


C. CANNIBALIZATION DETECTION (_detect_cannibalization)
-------------------------------------------------------
Algorithm:
  1. Take all products classified as "Growth" stage (velocity > 0.55)
  2. Take all products classified as "Declining" stage (velocity < 0.40)
  3. Cross-reference with MBA association rules (df_assoc_rules)
  4. Flag pairs (A, B) where:
        A is in "Growth"   AND
        B is in "Declining" AND
        A and B have an association rule A ↔ B with lift > 1.5

Why association rules? If product A and B are frequently bought together,
they serve similar or complementary use cases. If A is growing while B is
declining among the SAME customer base, A is likely replacing B.

Why this matters:
  - Stop re-ordering product B if A is replacing it
  - Redirect remaining B inventory via liquidation (connects to Inventory tab)
  - Use B's decline as a signal to negotiate better terms with B's supplier

Cannibalization flag output:
  growing_product   - the one rising
  declining_product - the one being displaced
  association_lift  - strength of their product relationship
  velocity_gap      - how far apart their scores are


D. END-OF-LIFE PREDICTION (_predict_end_of_life)
-------------------------------------------------
For every product in "Declining" stage, EOL is predicted using 4 signals:

D1. Revenue Extrapolation
  Using the fitted regression line:
    months_to_zero = -intercept / slope   (when the trend line crosses zero)
  
  If slope >= 0 or months_to_zero > 36: skip (not declining fast enough)

D2. Stock Age Penalty
  From view_ageing_stock: if this product has old unsold stock:
    age_factor = max_age_days / 180   (normalized: 180 days = max severity)
  Higher stock age → accelerates predicted EOL

D3. Buyer Attrition
  buyer_count in last 3 months vs same period 12 months ago:
    attrition_rate = 1 - (current_buyers / peak_buyers)
  High attrition → fewer partners carrying it → accelerating decline

D4. Peak Distance
  How far below the product's all-time peak revenue is it today?
  peak_distance = (peak_revenue - current_revenue) / peak_revenue

COMPOSITE EOL SCORE:
  eol_urgency = (0.45 × revenue_slope_severity)
              + (0.25 × buyer_attrition)
              + (0.20 × peak_distance)
              + (0.10 × age_factor)

Estimated months to EOL:
  = months_to_zero × (1 - 0.30 × eol_urgency)
  (Higher urgency shortens the estimate)

Urgency Labels:
  eol_months < 3   → "Critical" (act now)
  eol_months < 9   → "Warning" (plan phase-out)
  eol_months < 18  → "Watch" (monitor closely)


E. PRODUCT TREND DRILLDOWN (get_product_trend)
-----------------------------------------------
Returns the raw monthly revenue time series for a selected product.
Plotted as a line chart with:
  - Actual revenue per month (bars)
  - Trend regression line overlay (dashed)
  - Velocity score annotation
  - Lifecycle stage badge


DATA FLOW
---------
  PostgreSQL → transactions_dsr + transactions_dsr_products + master_products
             → _load_product_monthly_revenue (monthly pivot by group_name)
             → _compute_growth_velocity (regression + scoring)
             → _detect_cannibalization (cross with df_assoc_rules)
             → _predict_end_of_life (multi-factor EOL timeline)
             → Results cached in engine._lifecycle_velocity, _lifecycle_cannibal, _lifecycle_eol
             → product_lifecycle.py renders all three datasets


IMPROVEMENTS / SUGGESTIONS
---------------------------
1. PROPHET / TEMPORAL FUSION TRANSFORMER: Replace linear regression with
   Meta's Prophet or a deep learning forecaster (TFT) for better handling
   of seasonality, holidays, and sudden demand shocks.

2. COMPETITIVE SUBSTITUTION DATA: If a competitor launches a similar product,
   flag it as an external cannibalization risk (not just internal).

3. PRODUCT PROFITABILITY OVERLAY: Combine with margin data. A declining product
   with 35% margin is worth fighting for more than a growing one at 5%.

4. CUSTOMER COHORT ANALYSIS PER PRODUCT: Which type of partner buys this product?
   If only "Critical" health partners buy it, the product may decline further
   as those partners churn.

5. NEW PRODUCT INTRODUCTION TRACKING: Flag products in their first 6 months
   separately — they should not be penalized for having low revenue history.

6. SUPPLY CHAIN INTEGRATION: Connect EOL predictions to purchase orders.
   Automatically suggest "Do not reorder" for products in Critical EOL stage.

7. CROSS-ELASTICITY MATRIX: Compute price cross-elasticity between product pairs.
   Confirms whether cannibalization is price-driven or feature-driven.

8. MLflow EXPERIMENT TRACKING: Log each lifecycle computation run with thresholds
   and results so analysts can compare different sensitivity settings.
