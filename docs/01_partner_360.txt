PURPOSE
-------
Partner 360 is the core health dashboard for individual B2B partners. It gives
sales teams a single pane of glass showing exactly how healthy a partner is,
their churn risk, credit standing, revenue trend, and what product to pitch next.
It is designed for daily use by account managers before customer calls.


WHAT IT SHOWS
-------------
1. Health Status Card    - Overall health label (Healthy / At Risk / Critical)
2. Revenue Metrics       - Recent 90-day vs previous 90-day revenue, drop %
3. Churn Risk Score      - Probability (0-100%) the partner stops buying
4. Churn Risk Band       - Low / Medium / High based on configurable thresholds
5. Survival Analysis     - How many days until the partner is expected to churn
6. SHAP Explanation      - Top features driving this partner's churn risk
7. Credit Risk Score     - Payment behavior and outstanding amount risk
8. Forecast (30-day)     - Predicted revenue for next 30 days
9. Top Affinity Pitch    - Best product to cross-sell based on purchase patterns
10. Export (PDF / Excel) - Full partner report download


ALGORITHMS & FORMULAS USED
---------------------------

A. HEALTH SCORING (base_loader_mixin.py → _add_health_scores)
--------------------------------------------------------------
Health score is a weighted composite of 4 normalized signals:

  health_score = (0.35 × revenue_strength)
               + (0.30 × growth_trend)
               + (0.20 × recency_activity)
               + (0.15 × stability)

Where:
  revenue_strength  = normalize(log1p(recent_90_revenue))
  growth_trend      = normalize(growth_rate_90d clipped to [-1.0, 1.5])
  recency_activity  = 1 - normalize(recency_days)
  stability         = 1 - normalize(log1p(revenue_volatility))

All values are normalized to [0, 1] using min-max normalization across partners.

Health segments assigned from score:
  score >= 0.8 AND drop < 10%    → Champion (Healthy Growing)
  score >= 0.6 AND drop < threshold → Healthy (Stable)
  score >= 0.4                   → At Risk (Degrowth)
  recent_90 == 0 AND prev_90 > 0 → Critical (Churned Risk)
  score < 0.4                    → Critical (Immediate Action)

Revenue degrowth threshold is STATE-SPECIFIC:
  degrowth_threshold_pct = 70th percentile of positive revenue drops in that state
  (clipped between 10% and 40%)

This makes the system adaptive — a 20% drop in Tamil Nadu (competitive market)
may be normal, but in UP it flags as critical.


B. REVENUE DROP %
-----------------
  revenue_drop_pct = ((prev_90_revenue - recent_90_revenue) / prev_90_revenue) × 100
  Only calculated when prev_90_revenue > 0 AND recent_90_revenue < prev_90_revenue.


C. CHURN MODEL (churn_credit_mixin.py → _train_churn_model)
------------------------------------------------------------
Algorithm: LightGBM (Gradient Boosted Trees) with Platt scaling calibration
Fallback:  Logistic Regression (if LightGBM not installed)

TRAINING DATA CONSTRUCTION (_build_churn_training_data):
  Uses temporal sliding window approach. For each partner at multiple anchor dates:
  - Features computed from TRAILING window (the "past")
  - Label = did the partner have zero revenue in the NEXT horizon_days (90 days)?

Feature Set (12 features):
  1.  recent_90_revenue      - Revenue in last 90 days
  2.  prev_90_revenue        - Revenue in previous 90 days  
  3.  recent_txns            - Transaction count last 90 days
  4.  prev_txns              - Transaction count previous 90 days
  5.  recency_days           - Days since last purchase
  6.  growth_rate_90d        - (recent - prev) / prev revenue ratio
  7.  revenue_drop_pct       - % revenue decline
  8.  avg_order_value        - recent_90_revenue / recent_txns
  9.  aov_trend              - (current AOV - prev AOV) / prev AOV
  10. category_count         - Distinct product groups bought (last 90 days)
  11. category_diversity_change - category_count - category_count_prev
  12. engagement_velocity     - recent_txns / prev_txns (capped at 10)

Model pipeline:
  RobustScaler → LightGBMClassifier(n_estimators=300, learning_rate=0.05) → CalibratedClassifierCV

Calibration ensures the output probability is TRUE probability, not just a score.

Evaluation metrics tracked: ROC-AUC, Average Precision (PR-AUC)

Churn risk bands (configurable via env):
  churn_probability >= 0.65  → High Risk
  churn_probability >= 0.35  → Medium Risk
  churn_probability < 0.35   → Low Risk


D. SHAP EXPLAINABILITY (_compute_shap_importance)
-------------------------------------------------
After training, SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) values are computed:
- Global: bar chart of mean |SHAP| values per feature (feature importance)
- Per-partner: SHAP waterfall — which feature pushed risk UP or DOWN

SHAP formula (conceptually):
  For each feature i, SHAP value φ_i measures the average marginal contribution
  of feature i across all possible orderings of features, weighted by how much
  including feature i changes the prediction.

Why SHAP over feature importance?
  - Directional: shows if recency_days is INCREASING or DECREASING churn risk
  - Locally accurate: each partner's explanation sums to their exact prediction
  - Consistent: larger feature effects always get larger SHAP values


E. SURVIVAL ANALYSIS (_fit_survival_model)
------------------------------------------
Two models are fit:

E1. Kaplan-Meier (non-parametric)
  - Estimates probability of "not churning" at each time point
  - No assumptions about underlying distribution
  - Used for the survival curve visualization

E2. Cox Proportional Hazards Model (semi-parametric)
  h(t|X) = h₀(t) × exp(β₁x₁ + β₂x₂ + ... + βₙxₙ)
  
  Where:
    h(t|X)  = hazard rate at time t for partner with features X
    h₀(t)   = baseline hazard (estimated non-parametrically)
    β values = learned coefficients (how each feature affects churn speed)

  Median time-to-churn is estimated as the time when partner's survival
  probability crosses 0.50 on their individual survival curve.

Why survival analysis in addition to classification?
  A binary churn model tells you IF someone will churn.
  Survival analysis tells you WHEN — which helps prioritize outreach.


F. CREDIT RISK SCORING (_load_credit_risk_features, _score_credit_risk)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Input signals:
  - outstanding_amount: current unpaid invoices
  - overdue_ratio: proportion of invoices past due date
  - credit_utilization: outstanding / credit_limit (if available)

Credit risk score is a weighted combination:
  credit_risk_score = w1 × overdue_ratio + w2 × credit_utilization
                    + w3 × outstanding_amount_normalized

Risk bands:
  score >= 0.67 → High Credit Risk
  score >= 0.40 → Medium Credit Risk
  score < 0.40  → Low Credit Risk


G. REVENUE FORECAST (_build_partner_forecast)
---------------------------------------------
Simple exponential smoothing applied per partner using their monthly revenue history.
Forecast horizon: next 30 days.

forecast_next_30d = weighted average extrapolation of last N months
                    (more recent months weighted higher)

Why simple smoothing instead of ARIMA?
  Many partners have short or sparse history. Complex autoregressive models
  require minimum 24 months of data. Exponential smoothing works with 3-6 months.


DATA FLOW
---------
  PostgreSQL → view_ml_input (df_ml)
             → fact_sales_intelligence (df_fact, indexed by company_name)
             → _build_partner_features / _build_partner_features_from_views (df_partner_features)
             → ChurnCreditMixin: training data → model → predictions merged into df_partner_features
             → Partner 360 tab reads from df_partner_features.loc[selected_partner]


IMPROVEMENTS / SUGGESTIONS
---------------------------
1. PROPHET FORECASTING: Replace exponential smoothing with Meta's Prophet for
   better handling of seasonality (quarterly patterns, festival spikes in India).

2. ENSEMBLE CHURN MODEL: Combine LightGBM predictions with survival analysis
   hazard scores using a stacking layer for higher accuracy.

3. PAYMENT BEHAVIOR FEATURES: Add "payment regularity score" — standard deviation
   of days-to-pay across invoices. Irregular payers churn faster.

4. COHORT ANALYSIS: Show which "batch" (acquisition year) the partner belongs to
   and whether that cohort is collectively churning.

5. GEOGRAPHIC RISK OVERLAY: If multiple partners in the same city/pin are
   churning simultaneously, flag it as a territory-level risk.

6. REAL-TIME ALERTS: Push WhatsApp/email alert the moment a partner's churn
   probability crosses the High Risk threshold.

7. NPS / SATISFACTION SIGNAL: Integrate annual partner satisfaction survey scores
   as an input feature into the churn model.

8. COMPETITOR SIGNAL: If a competitor is underpricing in a specific state, flag
   partners in that state automatically as elevated churn risk.
