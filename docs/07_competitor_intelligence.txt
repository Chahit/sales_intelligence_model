PURPOSE
-------
Competitor Intelligence helps Consistent's sales team understand where their
competitors are beating them on PRICE for the same or equivalent products.

When a competitor is sell a substitute for Rs 850 while Consistent sells it
for Rs 1,000 — the partner is incentivized to switch supplier. This module
identifies those dangerous price gaps before partners start churning.

Additionally, it surfaces where Consistent has a PRICE ADVANTAGE — products
where our pricing is significantly better — which are natural upsell
opportunities ('we're cheaper than the competition here, stock up').


WHAT IT SHOWS
-------------
1. Competitor Summary         - How many competitors tracked, products compared
2. Undercut Products Table    - Products where competitors are cheaper
   - Product name, competitor name, their price, our price, % difference
3. Price Advantage Products   - Products where we are cheaper
4. Price Gap Distribution     - Histogram of competitive gaps
5. Regional Exposure          - Which states are most exposed to competitor undercutting
6. Partner Overlap            - Which of our partners also buy from competitors


ALGORITHMS & FORMULAS USED
---------------------------

A. PRICE COMPARISON
-------------------
For each product where competitor data exists:

  price_diff_abs = competitor_price - our_price
  price_diff_pct = (competitor_price - our_price) / our_price × 100

Interpretation:
  price_diff_pct < 0  → Competitor is CHEAPER (they are undercutting us)
  price_diff_pct > 0  → Competitor is MORE EXPENSIVE (we have price advantage)

"Undercut threshold" (configurable, default: -5%):
  Products where price_diff_pct < -5% are flagged as "actively being undercut"
  (5% is meaningful — below that, partners absorb it as goodwill/relationship premium)


B. UNDERCUT DETECTION (get_undercut_products)
---------------------------------------------
SQL/Pandas logic:
  1. Join our product catalog with competitor price table on product_name (or equivalent)
  2. Compute price_diff_pct per (our_product, competitor) pair
  3. Filter where price_diff_pct < min_diff_pct threshold
  4. Rank by how severely we're being undercut (most negative diff first)

Output per row:
  product_name:      Our product being undercut
  competitor_name:   Which competitor is cheaper
  our_price:         Our current selling price
  competitor_price:  Competitor's price for equivalent product
  price_diff_pct:    How much cheaper competitor is (%)
  state:             Where this price comparison applies (if regional data exists)


C. COMPETITIVE EXPOSURE SCORING
--------------------------------
For each partner, compute a "competitive exposure score":

  n_undercut_products = number of products partner buys where we're undercut
  avg_undercut_severity = mean price_diff_pct for those products
  
  exposure_score = n_undercut_products × |avg_undercut_severity| / 100

Partners with high exposure + high churn probability are DOUBLE risk:
  → They are price-sensitive AND being actively targeted by cheaper competitors
  → These partners get a "Competitive Risk" flag in Partner 360

Why this matters: A partner seeing 15% cheaper alternatives for 3 of their top
5 products is a near-certain churner — and the reason won't show up in the
purchase data until AFTER they've switched.


D. REGIONAL PRICE ANALYSIS
----------------------------
If competitor data includes state/region:
  Group undercut products by state
  Sum up exposure across all partners in that state
  
  state_exposure = Σ(partner_exposure × partner_revenue_weight)

Output: Heat map / table of states ranked by competitive vulnerability.

This helps the regional sales manager know which territory to defend first.


E. PRODUCT SUMMARY STATS (get_competitor_summary)
--------------------------------------------------
Returns high-level stats dict:
  our_product_count:   Total distinct products in our catalog tracked
  competitor_count:    Total distinct competitors monitored
  products_undercut:   Count with price_diff_pct < -5%
  products_advantage:  Count with price_diff_pct > +5%
  products_parity:     Count within ±5% (effectively same price)
  avg_undercut_pct:    Average severity where we're being undercut
  avg_advantage_pct:   Average advantage where we're cheaper


DATA FLOW
---------
  PostgreSQL → competitor_prices table (competitor_name, product_name, price, date)
             → our product catalog (product_name, our_price)
             → competitor_mixin: join + compute price_diff_pct
             → get_competitor_summary() → summary stats
             → get_undercut_products(min_diff_pct=-5.0) → undercut table
             → competitor_intel.py renders tables and charts


IMPROVEMENTS / SUGGESTIONS
---------------------------
1. AUTOMATED PRICE SCRAPING: Instead of manual competitor price entry, build
   a web scraper (or use an API from TradeIndia / IndiaMart) to automatically
   refresh competitor prices weekly.

2. PRICE ELASTICITY MODELING: For each product, estimate how much a 1% price
   reduction increases our order volume with price-sensitive partners.

3. DYNAMIC PRICING SUGGESTIONS: If we're 8% more expensive on a product,
   and that product has 0.45 gross margin, calculate the minimum discount
   that still maintains profitability while matching competitor partly.

4. ALERT SYSTEM: When a competitor drops their price more than 10% on any
   product overnight, auto-send a Slack/email alert to the product manager.

5. PARTNER-LEVEL PRICE SENSITIVITY: Track which partners ACTUALLY switched
   away when price gaps existed — this gives a real price-sensitivity score
   per partner type.

6. WIN/LOSS ANALYSIS: Link competitor intel to the recommendation feedback
   data. When a partner rejects a pitch, capture whether "price" was cited
   as the reason and map to competitor product.

7. BRAND PERCEPTION PREMIUM: Some partners will pay 10% more for a trusted
   brand. Quantify this "brand premium" per partner segment to know where
   price matching is unnecessary.

8. SUPPLIER NEGOTIATION SUPPORT: Use undercut data to negotiate better
   purchase prices with our own suppliers: "Competitor buys at X, we need
   to be at Y to compete — can you meet us at Y?"
